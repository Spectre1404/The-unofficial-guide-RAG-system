Typical Amenities Near UIC (Market Summary)

Included in many listings:
- Heat (especially older Chicago buildings)
- Water and trash

Usually extra:
- Electricity and gas
- Internet ($45–$70/month)
- Renter's insurance ($12–$20/month)

Common building features:
- Laundry in-unit or on-site
- Air conditioning (not universal in vintage buildings)
- Secure entry
- Package delivery areas

UHomes Tips for UIC Renters

1. Filter by "near campus" and CTA line if you won't have a car
2. Compare price per bed for shared units, not just total rent
3. Read utility notes — "low rent" with high electric can equal out
4. Book tours early for August; inventory peaks April–June
5. Cross-check listings on UIC Off-Campus Housing site for landlord-posted duplicates

Budget Estimates Per Student (Fall 2025, shared units)

Economy: $650–$850/month — Bridgeport or outer Pilsen, 3–4BR split
Mid-range: $850–$1,050/month — Pilsen, Little Italy shared, older UV walk-ups
Premium: $1,100–$1,400/month — solo studio, furnished corporate, Little Italy 2BR split

Before You Apply

- Prepare $50–$75 application fee per person per property
- Security deposit often equals one month's rent upfront
- First month's rent sometimes due at signing
- Proof of UIC enrollment strengthens application
- Groups should submit applications same day

Scam Warning

Never pay deposit via wire transfer to a landlord who won't meet you at the unit. Verify UHomes "verified" badge and cross-reference address on UIC Off-Campus Housing when possible.
