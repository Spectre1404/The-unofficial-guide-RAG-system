COMMUTER STUDENT RESOURCE CENTER (CSRC)
Off-Campus Housing Resources — University of Illinois Chicago

The Commuter Student Resource Center supports UIC students who live off campus or commute to the university. This page consolidates resources for finding housing, understanding leases, resolving landlord issues, managing roommate relationships, and knowing your rights as a tenant in Chicago.

Finding Off-Campus Housing

UIC students are encouraged to begin their housing search with the UIC Off-Campus Housing Listing Service, which features rentals targeted to the UIC community. Supplemental search tools may include reputable apartment listing websites and property management companies serving the Little Italy, University Village, Pilsen, and Medical District neighborhoods.

Before signing a lease:
- Visit the property in person when possible
- Confirm total monthly costs including utilities not covered by rent
- Research the neighborhood for safety, transit access, and grocery options
- Review the lease with CSRC staff or a trusted advisor if this is your first rental agreement

CSRC offers one-on-one appointments to discuss neighborhood selection based on your class locations, budget, and transportation needs.

Navigating Landlord Issues

Landlords in Chicago are subject to the Chicago Residential Landlord and Tenant Ordinance (RLTO). Common issues reported by UIC students include delayed repairs, pest infestations, heating failures in winter, security deposit disputes, and improper entry without notice.

Recommended steps:
1. Document the issue with dated photographs and written communication
2. Notify the landlord or property manager in writing (email is acceptable)
3. Allow reasonable time for repair as defined by lease and local law
4. Contact CSRC for guidance before withholding rent or breaking a lease
5. File a complaint with the Chicago Department of Buildings for serious code violations if landlord is unresponsive

CSRC does not provide legal representation but can refer students to legal aid organizations serving renters in Illinois.

Roommate Conflicts

Living with roommates is a common off-campus experience. Disagreements may involve cleanliness, noise, guests, shared expenses, or thermostat settings. Prevent conflict by discussing expectations before move-in and using a written roommate agreement covering chores, bills, and quiet hours.

When conflicts arise:
- Address concerns early in a direct conversation
- Use "I" statements and specific examples
- Revisit the roommate agreement and revise if needed
- Seek CSRC mediation resources for facilitated discussion
- Understand that all co-signers on a lease may be held responsible for rent even if one roommate moves out

If a roommate fails to pay rent or violates the lease, consult CSRC and review your lease's joint liability clauses before taking action.

Lease Advice

Key lease terms UIC students should understand:

Security Deposit: Illinois law limits certain deposit practices; Chicago requires itemized deposit return within specified timelines after move-out.

Lease Term: Most off-campus leases run 12 months. Subletting may require landlord consent.

Utilities: Clarify which utilities are tenant responsibility. Heat inclusion is especially important in Chicago winters.

Renewal and Termination: Note automatic renewal clauses and required notice periods.

Condition of Unit: Complete a move-in checklist with photos to document pre-existing damage.

Subleasing: Verify that your lease permits subletting and obtain written approval.

Renter's Insurance: Strongly recommended to protect personal property and provide liability coverage. Some landlords require proof of insurance.

CSRC maintains a lease glossary and sample questions to ask landlords during property tours.

Tenant Rights in Chicago

Chicago renters have protections under city ordinance and state law, including:

- Right to habitable housing (heat, water, structural safety)
- Restrictions on retaliatory eviction after good-faith complaints
- Entry notice requirements for landlord access
- Security deposit return procedures
- Protections against lockouts without legal process

Students with disabilities may request reasonable accommodations in housing. Contact UIC Disability Resource Center for coordination with off-campus needs.

Income discrimination: Chicago prohibits discrimination based on lawful source of income, including housing vouchers, in most rental situations.

Emergency Housing Resources

Students facing sudden lease termination, domestic violence, or uninhabitable conditions should contact CSRC immediately. UIC Dean of Students office may assist with interim housing referrals. Chicago Metropolitan Battered Women's Network and City of Chicago rental assistance programs serve eligible residents — CSRC can provide contact information.

Commuter Life

Off-campus students are commuters. CSRC lounge offers microwaves, lockers, and study space between classes. Connect with other commuters through student organizations — social support matters when you're not in a residence hall.

Graduate and Professional Students

Graduate students often prioritize quiet housing and shorter commutes to research labs. CSRC advisors help weigh Medical District proximity against Pilsen value. Students with families should disclose occupancy accurately to landlords — fair housing protections apply.

International Students

Visa status does not prevent off-campus leasing. Landlords may require additional documentation. Maintain copies of all lease pages. Understand that breaking a lease can have immigration advising implications if it affects enrollment — consult OIS when needed.

Workshops

CSRC hosts quarterly "Know Your Rights as a Chicago Renter" sessions with community legal partners. Attendance is free for UIC students. Materials include sample roommate agreements and move-in inspection forms.
