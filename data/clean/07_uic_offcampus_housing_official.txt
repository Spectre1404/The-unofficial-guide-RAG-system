University of Illinois Chicago — Off-Campus Housing Office

Welcome to the UIC Off-Campus Housing Listing Service

The University of Illinois Chicago provides this listing service as a resource to help members of the UIC community locate rental housing in Chicago and the surrounding area. The Off-Campus Housing Office, part of Student Affairs, maintains an online database of rental properties, roommate requests, and sublease opportunities submitted by property owners, management companies, and UIC students.

This service is not a real estate brokerage. UIC does not negotiate leases, collect rent, or guarantee the accuracy of listings. Students and employees are encouraged to exercise due diligence when contacting landlords and signing rental agreements.

Who Can Use This Service

Students: All currently enrolled UIC students with an active UIC NetID may create an account, search listings, post roommate profiles, and advertise subleases.

Faculty and Staff: UIC faculty and staff members may use the listing service to search for rental housing and post available units if they are landlords.

Landlords and Property Managers: Owners and authorized agents may register to post rental listings. Registration requires agreement to UIC posting guidelines, including accurate pricing, availability, and contact information. Listings that violate fair housing laws or contain discriminatory language will be removed.

Non-UIC individuals: The service is restricted to the UIC community. Prospective tenants without a UIC affiliation cannot create accounts. However, listings may be shared informally by UIC users.

How to Search Listings

1. Visit the Off-Campus Housing portal through the UIC Off-Campus Housing website.
2. using your UIC NetID and password.
3. Select "Search Listings" from the main menu.
4. Apply filters including: neighborhood, number of bedrooms, price range, date available, furnished or unfurnished, utilities included, parking, and pet policy.
5. View listing details including photographs, property description, lease term, and landlord contact information.
6. Contact landlords directly via the email or phone provided. UIC does not intermediate communications.

Additional features include a map view of available properties near the UIC East and West campuses, saved searches, and optional email notifications when new listings match your criteria.

Roommate Matching

The Roommate Matching board allows UIC students to post profiles describing their housing preferences, budget, preferred neighborhoods, and lifestyle information. Students seeking roommates or available rooms in shared apartments may browse profiles and contact matches directly. UIC does not conduct background checks on users. Meet in public locations and verify identity before signing joint leases.

Subleases

UIC students who wish to sublet their apartment during study abroad, internships, or summer breaks may post sublease advertisements. Sublessors must comply with their original lease terms regarding subletting. Obtain written landlord approval where required. Sublessees should confirm that the primary tenant has authorization to sublet.

For Landlords: How to Post a Listing

Registered landlords may submit listings through the landlord portal. Required information includes property address, unit type, monthly rent, security deposit amount, lease start date, amenities, and at least one photograph. Listings remain active for 60 days and may be renewed. UIC reserves the right to edit or remove listings that are outdated, fraudulent, or non-compliant with posting policies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is there a fee for students to use the listing service?
A: No. Searching listings, posting roommate profiles, and advertising subleases is free for UIC students with an active NetID.

Q: How is this different from Campus Housing?
A: Campus Housing manages UIC residence halls on campus. The Off-Campus Housing Listing Service advertises private rentals in the community. Different offices, different contracts, different costs.

Q: Can graduate students and students with families use the service?
A: Yes. Filters include larger units and neighborhoods beyond the immediate campus radius. Family-friendly listings are tagged when landlords provide that information.

Q: What should I do if a listing appears fraudulent?
A: Do not send money. Contact the Off-Campus Housing Office with the listing ID and description. UIC may remove the listing and notify other users.

Q: Do landlords have to accept U-Pass holders without parking?
A: Parking is property-specific. Most near-campus buildings do not include parking in base rent. Ask before signing.

Workshops and Events

Each spring the Off-Campus Housing Office partners with CSRC to host "Apartment Hunting 101" workshops covering search timelines, lease review, and scam avoidance. A virtual landlord fair in April features property managers from Little Italy, University Village, and Pilsen. Check the Student Affairs events calendar for dates.

International Student Note

Students on F-1 or J-1 visas may use the listing service. Landlords may request visa documentation or U.S. co-signers. The Office of International Services can provide enrollment verification letters. UIC does not act as a lease guarantor.
