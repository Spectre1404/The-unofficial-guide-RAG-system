On-Campus Residence Life — University of Illinois Chicago

Welcome to Campus Housing

UIC Campus Housing provides on-campus living in university residence halls for undergraduate and graduate students. Living on campus offers proximity to classes, furnished rooms, structured community programming, and inclusive utilities in most rate plans. Applications are submitted through the Campus Housing portal.

Residence Halls

UIC operates multiple residence hall communities on and adjacent to the East Campus:

- Academic & Residential Complex (ARC): Large mixed community with study lounges, dining, and recreation space. Popular with first-year students.
- James Stukel Towers: Double and single rooms with shared floor amenities.
- Marie Robinson Hall: Community-oriented hall with shared common areas.
- Single Student Residence (SSR): Apartment-style units for upper-division and graduate students seeking more independent living on campus.
- Chicago Hall and other specialty assignments as available.

Room assignments consider application date, preferences, and program requirements. Gender-inclusive housing options are available upon request.

Room Styles and Rates

Room types include:
- Traditional double occupancy rooms
- Single rooms (limited availability, higher rate)
- Triple rooms (select halls)
- Apartment-style units in SSR with kitchen facilities

Rates vary by hall, occupancy, and meal plan selection. Published rates include fall and spring semesters. Summer housing is offered separately for students enrolled in summer courses or approved programs.

A typical double room with a standard meal plan represents a higher total cost than sharing an off-campus apartment with roommates in neighborhoods such as Pilsen or Bridgeport. However, on-campus housing bundles utilities, internet, maintenance, and programming into one bill, which simplifies budgeting.

Meal Plans

UIC Dining meal plans are required for most residents in traditional halls. Plans include a combination of meal swipes at dining centers and dining dollars for retail locations. Plans range from standard to premium swipe counts. Students with dietary restrictions may consult UIC Dining for accommodation support.

Meal plans are a significant portion of on-campus cost. Off-campus students prepare meals at home, which may reduce expenses for budget-conscious households.

Housing Contracts

The housing contract is a binding agreement for the academic year. Key terms include:
- Contract dates aligned with academic calendar
- Cancellation and withdrawal policies with financial penalties
- Guest and visitation guidelines
- Community conduct standards
- Room change request procedures
- Break housing availability (Thanksgiving, winter, spring — varies by hall)

Read the contract carefully before accepting assignment. Off-campus leases operate under Illinois landlord-tenant law and may offer different flexibility for summer breaks.

Application Process

1. Submit Campus Housing application via the housing portal
2. Pay application fee
3. Select hall and room type preferences
4. Receive assignment notification
5. Complete roommate matching questionnaire if applicable
6. Confirm assignment and submit deposit

Priority deadlines apply for guaranteed housing for incoming first-year students. Apply by published priority date for best selection.

On-Campus vs. Off-Campus Comparison

On-Campus Advantages:
- Walking distance to most East Campus facilities
- No separate utility accounts or furniture purchases
- Residential Life programming and peer support
- Maintenance handled by university staff
- Security desk in many halls

On-Campus Considerations:
- Higher total cost for many students compared to shared off-campus housing
- Less independent living space
- Break closure periods in some halls
- Meal plan requirements

Off-Campus Advantages:
- Potential cost savings with roommates in neighborhoods like Little Italy, University Village, or Pilsen
- Greater independence and choice of neighborhood
- Year-round lease control (subject to lease terms)
- Kitchen and living space flexibility

Off-Campus Considerations:
- Commute via walking, CTA, or bike
- Responsible for utilities, internet, and renters insurance
- Independent lease negotiation and landlord relations
- See UIC Off-Campus Housing and CSRC resources

Many upper-division students transition to off-campus housing after one or two years on campus. UIC does not require students to live on campus after the first year unless participating in specific programs.

What to Bring

On-campus rooms include bed frame, mattress, desk, chair, and closet or wardrobe in most configurations. Students typically bring bedding, towels, desk lamp, toiletries, clothing, and personal items. Kitchen equipment is not needed in traditional halls with meal plans. SSR apartment residents should coordinate cookware and small appliances with roommates.

Break and Summer Housing

Winter break: Many halls remain open for students registered for winter session or approved stay requests. Thanksgiving break may have modified access depending on hall policy.

Summer: Separate application required. Rates differ from academic-year contracts. Students taking summer courses, conducting research, or interning locally may apply. Students who live off campus in summer often sublease Chicago apartments or return home.

Disability and Accessibility

Accessible rooms and accommodations are available. Submit requests through Campus Housing and the Disability Resource Center as early as possible. Off-campus students with accessibility needs should consult CSRC for landlord accommodation guidance under fair housing law.

Parent and Family Information

Family members cannot sign the housing contract on behalf of adult students, but may serve as financial guarantors for off-campus leases. On-campus contracts are between the student and UIC. Family tours and information sessions are offered during orientation.
